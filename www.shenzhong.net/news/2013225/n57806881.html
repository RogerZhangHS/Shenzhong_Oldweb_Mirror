DYNAMIC ANALYSIS ON THE MOTION OF DRAGON BOARD & FLYING TURTLE

深圳中学 2013-02-25

                                                                                                               IC20 Yuankang Zhang
                                                                                       (该论文参加了USAYPT2013年度锦标赛，深圳中学在该比赛中获得一等奖。)

 What's the question?
How is the motion of these boards achieved and maintained in both
devices on the level ground?
 
Refine the question
What kind of force is driving the boards?
How do these boards move?
What is the gait of this movement?
Why do they move in this trail?
What are the elements which can influence this motion?
 
Solution
Dragon board is a new sport devices developes from skate board. But different from skate board, dragon board does not need rider to drive the ground. Rider just needs to wiggle his body but not to give a force forward. How can the forces which have a right angle with heading direction drive the board? Moreover, according to the conservation of linear momentum, internal forces cannot change the motion of the whole system, then where the driving force comes from? These are the question I would like to discuss in the following paragraphs.
                             
                   
 Dragon board can be separated to two different parts which connected by an axis of rotation , from which we can see two decks can rotate limitedly. Two tires which have enough friction are installed on decks with connect of steel shafts, tires can rotate around the shafts in 360°but it is also limited when rider drives the board. What's special,the extension of the shafts does not cross the touchpoints between the board and ground which means incline of decks can cause rotation of tires to cause rolling friction; when the board is accelerated forward, one component of rolling friction points to heading direction.
                                                        
 
  The normal force imposes on the tire-shaft system generates a torque which can give an angular acceleration to the shaft. We can calculate this acceleration and then get the relationship between rotation angle and time. Net frictional force is perpendicular to the tire so we can determine the driving force which equals to frictional force times to sinθ.
                                                              
 
  Moreover, we can analyze the motion of boards in the view of energy. When we look at the moving dragon board and the flying turtle, it’s not difficult to find out that dragon board causes obvious height difference: the position of the boards’ surfaces reach the highest point when it’s in equilibrium pattern and then it naturally changes to the lowest point. When the position is lowest, riders have to produce energy for the board and help the position of the boards’ surface change back to the height point. And one component of fictional force between the wheel and ground loses some energy. That means, if riders keep producing the energy for the dragon board, this part of energy become the potential energy, then the board change it into kinetic energy which keep the board moving. But what about the flying turtle? Indeed, flying turtle does not have obvious height difference, but that does not mean riders don’t produce energy for it or it’s energy forms never change. When we are rotating the steering wheel, we are already doing work, so the similar theory can be applied on the Flying Turtle.
