The Utilization of Solar and Wind Energy in Finland and China

深圳中学 2013-01-25

                                                                                                                    参与学生：   曾妮 刘佳璐 钟超平 许富荣
                                                                                                       刘晨露 桂欣玫 陈鸿胜 程瑞祥
As people begin to realize the significance of protecting the environment, the utilizations of clean energy such as solar and wind energy have been put in agenda. Except the protection of environment, the operation could bring attractive financial returns as well. Many countries such as Finland and China have made effort in the domain on different industries and through different approaches.
In the trend of environmental friendly utilization of energy, solar energy plays an important role. According to Wikipedia, solar energy is abundant that “the total solar energy absorbed by Earth's atmosphere, oceans and land masses is approximately 3,850,000 exajoules (EJ) per year.” (http://www.wiki.com) Countries all over the world are starting to exploit and use solar energy in many areas such as agriculture, horticulture, water treatment and industry. Finland, a developed county in Northern Europe and China, an influential developing country in East Asia, have already taken action in solar energy utilization.
The data shows that the objective in solar heating in Finland is 163 000 m2 collector area (1995–2010). The collector areas as well as installations of solar energy grow in recent years. (http://www.wiki.com) Beneq, a supplier of production and research equipment for advanced thin film coatings in Finland, “offers thin film coating equipment suitable for various types of solar cells.” The expertise of the company believes that the utilization of solar energy will not only be a challenge but also an opportunity. It is the efficiency way of improving the economy and environmental protection. (http://www.goodnewsfinland.com)
                              
                                                Beneq (http://www.goodnewsfinland.com)
Sava-Solar, another Finnish high-technical company, has made the similar progress. Based on the recent news, “they have developed a new type of solar power collector and the system employs direct flow technology, allowing water to circulate in the absorber itself, which can reduce the loss of energy and improve the heat transfer.” (http://news.xinhuanet.com) More importantly, the building materials of Finnish pavilion in Shanghai Expo in 2010 also make use of solar energy. The solar cell panel on the roof can produce electricity for air-conditioners in sweltering summer. (http://www.finlandatexpo2010.fi)
    On the other side, “China is a world leader in the manufacture of solar photovoltaic technology, with its six biggest solar companies having a combined value of over $15 billion,” according to Wikipedia. “The government has announced plans to expand the installed capacity to 20 GW by 2020.” (http://www.wiki.com) For instance, Kaohsiung National Stadium in Taiwan uses solar energy for the operation of the stadium and save some extra electricity.
                                                                    
                                                                                     Kaohsiung National Stadium (http://en.wikipedia.org)
Moreover, China has companies which concentrate on solar energy development like some in Finland as well. The Himin Solar Thermal Corp is a corporation among them. The firm has created a community with diverse infrastructure like schools, hospitals, factories and hotels which locates in Dezhou, Shandong Province. It is important that the infrastructure is completely powered by solar energy. Huang Ming, the chairman said that “now we put up our own buildings and have had good sales so far. And this success has attracted many developers.” (http://www.china.org)
 Another energy that is significant to the environmental protection is wind energy, one kind of renewable, clean and plentiful energy, is now widely used all over the world. It is a new energy that is originated from the solar radiation and produces no greenhouse gas emission during operation. According to a research, although only 2% of solar energy transform into wind energy, it is still a huge amount, which is ten times larger than the amount hydropower on earth. By 2008, worldwide wind-generated electricity is about 94.1 million kilowatts, supplied power more than 1% of world consumption. Wind power, as an unpolluted way to produce electricity, is very potential, especially to somewhere that owns poor traffic such as rural places and costal towns.
                                                         
   
    Wind turbines (http://www.vtt.fi/research/technology/wind_power.jsp?lang=en)
Obviously, wind power is one of the best choices for human to implement sustainable development because of its intrinsic advantages. Many countries such as Finland and China have had a complete system of wind energy’s utilization. According to the data on VTT Technical Research Centre of Finland, one of the biggest multitechnological applied research organizations in Northern Europe, wind power in Finland was 143 MW with 118 turbines in December 2008.The capacity of wind power is (2006) 86 MW. This is 0.2 % of electricity consumption.However, wind power could produce at least 20–30 % of Finland's energy demand, which means that there is a considerable potency for the development of wind energy in Finland. (http://www.vtt.fi/index.jsp) Wind power is one of the fastest growing areas of power production globally, contributing to the mitigation of climate change and to the diversification of energy supply. The VTT Technical Research Center believes that the Finnish wind technology industry has an important place in the global value chain. In the coming decade, the use of wind for electricity generation is likely to also emerge in Finland, requiring R&D to help deployment and address the technological challenges of Finnish climate conditions, especially offshore.
On the other side, China is taking the renewable energy projects at a fast pace. According to the Wikipedia, wind power in the People's Republic of China accounted for(2010) 41.8 gig watts (GW) of electricity generating capacity, becoming the largest wind energy provider worldwide. (www.wikipedia.com)What is more, Researchers from Harvard and Tsinghua University have found that China could meet all of their electricity demands from wind power through 2030.Construction of the first offshore wind farm in China started in April 2009, close to Shanghai Dongdaqiao, which is scheduled to be completed by the end of 2009 and to provide electricity to the 2010 Shanghai Expo. The wind farm will consist of 34 wind turbines, each of 3 MW capacities.
Furthermore, there are several companies in China which consider the wind energy development as the prime business such as Goldwind from Xinjiang province. These companies own many wind farms in some areas that are abundant wind sources such as Hainan Province near Pacific Ocean. Megan Treacy, one of the most authoritative commentators, says that China could replace coal with wind energy, because the meteorological and financial forecasts show that China can run on wind alone. (http://ecogeek.org/wind-power/2948-china-could-replace-coal-with-wind)
        A Goldwind farm outside of Urumqi,Xinjiang, (http://en.wikipedia.org)
Indeed, the solar energy and wind energy will become the main energy in the future. As the consumption of energy is ascending, the utilization of wind energy and solar energy will be more comprehensive and effective. Although the solar energy and wind energy will be almost endless, we still need to save energy in our daily life, because saving energy is also a crucial way of energy utilization.
