Electromagnetic induction and our life

深圳中学 2013-01-25

                                                             参与学生：     曾妮 刘佳璐 钟超平 许富荣

                                                                                         （中芬项目物理论文）
                     
It is said that human beings discovered the phenomenon of magnet and electric respectively hundreds of years ago. For instance, working people of ancient china invented the compass; Franklin did research about electric. Nevertheless, people started unit them together ,Since Michael Faraday is generally credited with the discovery of Electromagnetic induction in 1831.

On account of Electromagnetism is one of the four fundamental interactions in nature, some People live under the illusion that Electromagnetic induction separates a remote distance from our daily lives. That kind of point view was supposed to be wrong.
 
 
On the present, I suppose that the usage of Electromagnetic induction plays an important role in our daily life.
 
Subsequently, I will present two examples of Electromagnetic inductions in our life.
First, I will introduce the good helper of cooking, induction cooker, which named after the Electromagnetic induction.
An induction cooker uses induction heating for cooking. Unlike other forms of cooking, heat is generated directly in the pot or pan (cooking vessel), as opposed to being generated in the stovetop by electrical coils or burning gas.
                              
    
How Induction Cooking Works:
1.     The element's electronics power a coil (the red lines) that produces a high-frequency electromagnetic field (represented by the orange lines).
2.     That field penetrates the metal of the ferrous (magnetic-material) cooking vessel and sets up a circulating electric current, which generates heat. (But see the note below.)
3.     The heat generated in the cooking vessel is transferred to the vessel's contents.
4.     Nothing outside the vessel is affected by the field--as soon as the vessel is removed from the element, or the element turned off, heat generation stops.
(Image courtesy of Induction Cooking World)
 
Moreover, the Electromagnetic induction has been widely used in the area of electric power generation. The generation of the reservoir, even the nuclear energy power generation both utilize the theory of Electromagnetic induction.
 
In the years of 1831–1832,Michael Faraday discovered the operating principle of electromagnetic generators. The principle, later called Faraday's law, is that an electromotive force is generated in an electrical conductor that encircles a varying magnetic flux.

Temporarily, the urban civilization cannot live without the electrics and the variety of electromagnetic generators. Just imagine one day that the world without electromagnetic generators, the factory might stop working, equity market cannot trade normally, all of the electrical appliances fell into sleep…People may regard that day as the doomsday.
 
Electromagnetic induction is not such far from us, it is a member of our everyday life.
