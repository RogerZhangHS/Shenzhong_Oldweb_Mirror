American Elk

深圳中学 2013-01-25

                                            参与学生：吴家豪  朱子宁 翟爽 张宝伦

Abstract
A group of elks are reintroduced into the Great Smokey Mountain National Park (GSMAP), with the data within the first ten years known as premise. We come up with 3 models to predict future’s population.
In the first and second model, we make use of regression functions on morality and birth rate, and thus came up with stochastic polynomial models of population which show the tendency that the population will rise in the future.
In the third model, we introduce the PVA (Population Viability Analysis) method, and keep the basic usage of linear regression function of the instantaneous increasing rate, and calculated the maximum capacity of the GSMAP for elks. We also calculated the possible time that the elk population comes to its extinction: .
In addition, some unpredictable factors such as natural or artificial catastrophes are not fully presented in the models, but the models are relatively persuasive. At last we listed a few ways to improve the living condition of the elks overtime.
 
Key words: GSMAP, regression function, differential equation, PVA, extinction possibility, population, dynamic forecast, improvement

1.1 Restatement:
        There is a study for Eastern Elk (Cervus canadensis canadiensis)'s living situation in the Western U.S for the prediction on the happening of the Manitoba Elk (cervus canadensis manitobensis) if are moved to the Eastern U.S, specifically to the Great Smokey Mountain National Park to adapt. Here, carefully and detailedly studying and recording the Commissioner of the Department of Wild life enlists a all items that impact the population of the Eastern Elk (in short as "elk" in the following), including the number respectively of the reintroduced, births, death, poached, sickness, accident, predator, unknown, and total population in each year (according to the label in the table given with Problem A). We classify these quantities into four sorts with respect to years number as independent variable: the young (birth, all elks that have no ability to reproduce), the dead (death), the total population, and the adult (total population - (minus) the death - the birth), which is a quantity that we develop to find an direct relationship with the birth next year, since the birth in that year cannot bear a baby the next year obviously.
       In fact, these quantities affect each other simultaneously or in a year later, within a specific relationship or coefficient. With the knowledge of the demography and biology of the elk, we find that the number of births correlates with the number of adults of the front year. The number of deaths relates to the number of the total population of the front year. And both of the qualities that show the change in elks' amount (the birth and the death) impact the amount of the total population that determines the death in the next year, ideally. With a year passes, the ages of the elks increase in one may introduce more elks to have the ability to reproduce, but have some adult elks that have the ability to reproduce dead.
       Here we try to analyze the trend of the population changing as the basic development from 2001 to 2011 and condition are fixed in our model. Further, we would figure out the correlation that each kind of lethality affects on the number of death and even on the total population. With the learning in the differences of the circumstance in East and West, limit or even eliminate the activity of some lethal factors to make better prediction of Manitoba Elk's exchange in Great Smokey Mountain National Park.
 
1.2 Assumption
1.       We assume that every elk is identical except for the gender or some other aspects.
2.       The genders of the elk each weights 50% in amount.
3.       The deaths of the elks are all the adults.
4.       Each elk lives for 12 years. And each elk is able to reproduce from 3 to 12. We assume that each pair of elks has a fixed rate of reproducing.
5.       A female adult reproduce a young elk in each two 2 years (actually re-ensuring the biological knowledge)
6.       We assume that each female elk is able to give birth to no more than one young elk each year.
7.       There are five factors that affect the annual death toll: poaching, sickness, accident, predator, and unknown factors. We assume that there is a function that can describe the relationship between population and annual death toll.
8.       We assume that we calculate the sum of elks once a year on a fixed date.

3.4 Under what circumstances will the elks extinct?
   
Above are the models we get under the assumption that there will be no epidemic, or war, or grouped poachers. However the models are not persuasive enough. In fact, there are many factors that can’t be forecasted that affect the elks population.
If there is an epidemic after 2015 which cause over 150 deaths, the elks population is likely to extinct because the remaining no more than 3 elks are not likely to survive.
If there is a war in the 21th century in which the GSMNP is involved, there will maybe no more enough food or necessary protection for the elks. The extinction possibility is likely to have a sharp rise.
As the globe becomes warmer and warmer and the human population becoming larger, there will be increasing needs for resources and lands. One day, if the US changed the policy and made the GSNMP into a factory, the elks will surely not survive.
Anyway, the Population Variety Analysis has a poor credibility though each of the mathematic models shows that the elks are not likely to extinct.
 
  1. Improvements and conclusion
That the effect of these adaptations the introduction is ought to be found out. More specifically, mathematical models come out to decide whether the species will exist or die out. The following is what our research achieves.
We summarize the resistances against the increase of the amount of a species into two big parts: deterministic factors and stochastic factors. Deterministic factors refer to the ecological factors affecting on the species during the process of natural succession, while stochastic factors include environmental stochasticity, demographic stochasticity, natural catastrophes and genetic stochasticity. So what we should do now is to decrease these resistances using our findings in order to improve the growth of the Elk.
Deterministic factors are usually caused by unavoidable, fierce variations and external forces (such as glacial period), disappearance of foods and habitats, or the introduction of some lethal things (such as natural enemies and diseases). As for solutions, we should at least protect their habitats and, what’s more, we can change the environment of their habitats to make it more suitable for their living. We can also remove some of their natural enemies to other places so as to reach the aim of protecting these endangered species. In addition, we can remove this species to safer places when series diseases strike there origin habitats to avoid the illness.
Environmental stochasticity involves environment capacity, climate, relationship of competition, predation, parasitism and so on. Solutions to this part are almost the same as the above.
Demographic stochasticity is produced by differences between different individuals.
Natural catastrophes can lead to extinction of some species, so we should pay more attention to it. Actually, natural catastrophe is a special kind of environmental stochasticity, and the solutions are also the same.
Genetic stochasticity is the genetic characteristics of the random variation. Genetic drift and inbreeding depression may cause the disappearance of gene diversity. So we can try to prevent them from inbreeding.
 
 
Letter
Dear Mr. / Mrs. Who may read this letter:
To help determine the living situation and predict the future direction of the species of the elks reintroduced into the Great Smokey Mountain National Park (GSMAP), we analyze the data of the past 10 years and model the population of the elks under a conventional assumption that something catastrophic will not happen. What's more, we also include the analysis due to the huge disaster that may or may not cause the species to die out.
According to our modeling, the population will tortuously increase. But the general trend is an s-shape growth. The rate of the growth firstly increases and then decreases. That's why the graphs of the modeling results a curve bending and changing the direction it concave to.
Why would this kind of pattern occur? As we know, biologically, as the population grows towards a "full-speed", some living factors directly or indirectly become more and more difficult to endure such quickly growing species. That is, some potential threats occur and become more and more apparent. The factors, such environment, resources, even gene evolution lead the species changing from fertile to a little bit barren. "Full-speed" is reached but left but a deceleration starts to affect the speed of the population growth.
After all, the population grows. But the existence of the species may not be that easily predicted as the growing tendency is, because the unknown factors, aspects are too much, too powerful and too capricious. Actually, under a certain scale and strength sudden crisis, the elks can keep the species remaining. What's more, with the certain reduction of the population, the vitality may regain and stimulate the species to be boom and prosperous.
Therefore, we shall keep paying attention to the elks' living condition and the environment factors and changes surrounding and some artificial events related or happens nearby, but try not to interfere too much. Since somewhat threats to the species are needed to keep their wild nature, vigilance and stimulates the fertility of the species.
Here we thank the Department of Wildlife to protect the animals and keep the nature more than the amount of the animals, but the experiments and studies for scientific research and develop the better strategy for the following people to raise animals and give them a better condition to life as the truly wild ones do. We thank for your hard work and also thank you for reading this letter.
